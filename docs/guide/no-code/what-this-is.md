# What this is (in plain words)

Imagine your assistant kept a small **notebook** for your project. Every time you
made a decision, learned a fact, or left a question open, it jotted that down.
The next time you sat down — tomorrow, next week, in a brand-new chat — it could
flip the notebook open and remember exactly where you were.

That notebook is what KB Factory gives you. It's a **memory for your project**
that your AI assistant writes to and reads from, so your work carries across
conversations instead of evaporating when the chat ends.

## What it remembers for you

You can ask it to remember five kinds of things. You don't have to label them —
just say them naturally, and the assistant sorts them out:

- **Decisions** — "We'll release every Friday."
- **Facts** — "The signup form has a 5-step limit."
- **Assumptions** — "We're assuming most users are on mobile."
- **Open questions** — "We haven't picked a name yet."
- **Lessons learned** — "Skipping the review step caused last month's mix-up."

Later, instead of scrolling back through old chats, you just **ask**: *"What did
we decide about the release schedule?"* — and it answers from the notebook.

## The one thing that makes it special

Most assistant "memory" works like a whiteboard: when something changes, the old
note gets wiped and the new one takes its place. Handy — but the *history*
disappears.

KB Factory works like a notebook you only ever **add to**. When a decision
changes, it doesn't erase the old one. It writes the new decision and quietly
links it to the one it replaced. So you can always answer the question that a
whiteboard can't: *"Wait — why did we change our minds about this?"*

That's the whole point. If keeping that trail matters to you, this is worth it.

## Is this actually for you?

Honestly? **Not always.** Here's a straight answer:

- **You'll love it if** you want your project's decisions and reasoning to *stick*
  across many conversations — and you care about being able to look back and see
  how your thinking changed over time.
- **You probably don't need it if** you just want a couple of light reminders for
  your assistant and you don't care about history. In that case a simple notes
  file (and your assistant's own built-in memory) is easier and perfectly fine.

There's a longer, even-handed version of this trade-off in the
[comparison](../../comparison.md) — but the short version is above.

## Where it lives, and what you'll need

- Everything stays **on your own computer**, inside your project. There's no
  account to create, no subscription, no internet required once it's set up.
- You use it through **Claude** — either **Claude Cowork** (the desktop app,
  which is the easiest for non-developers) or **Claude Code**.
- You'll add a small **plugin** once, and from then on you just talk.
